About Me

I am a second-year architecture student, deeply passionate about designing spaces that inspire and serve humanity. Every structure I envision is driven by a commitment to functionality, aesthetics, and human-centered design, as I believe architecture has the power to shape experiences and improve lives.

Beyond design, I am also a dedicated researcher, constantly seeking knowledge to innovate and solve real-world challenges. My curiosity pushes me to explore new ideas, materials, and sustainable solutions that bridge creativity with practicality.

Art has always been a vital part of my expression—I am a painter, using colors and textures to capture emotions and tell stories. Whether on canvas or in sketches, my work reflects my architectural mindset, blending form and meaning.

As a voiceover artist, I bring words to life, whether in narrations, presentations, or creative projects. My voice is another tool I use to communicate ideas, evoke emotions, and connect with audiences in a meaningful way.

Lastly, I am a committed volunteer, believing that true impact comes from serving others. Whether through community projects, advocacy, or sharing my skills, I strive to make a difference—one small act at a time.

My goal is simple: to leave a positive mark through architecture, research, art, voice, and service, creating a legacy that goes beyond myself.

Architecture

Architect's House & Studio

This house and studio for an architect utilizes interlocking geometric forms to create dynamic spaces and maximize natural light. A neutral palette of blue, gray, and wood is accented with pops of color to stimulate creativity. The material palette includes exposed concrete for strength and a raw aesthetic, large expanses of glass for transparency, and warm wood accents for comfort.

Residential Workspace Modern

Cancer Care Center

This sketch presents the exterior of my cancer care center design – a serene, structured composition where clean geometries frame a deliberate connection to nature. The building's rectilinear forms create sheltered outdoor spaces and carefully composed views, allowing patients to engage with natural elements through architecture that feels protective yet open. Strategic openings and material transitions subtly guide movement while maintaining a sense of calm dignity.

Art

Sufi Soul in Contemplation

Medium: Acrylic on canvas

Dimensions: 20×20 cm

This painting depicts a Sufi figure suspended in a moment of profound existential questioning, embodying the tension between divine mystery and human yearning. The brushwork merges fluidity with tension, mirroring the themes of duality in the accompanying poetic text: "Are you light or fire? / Gentle or compelled? / Your 99 names at war— / Does victory belong to Hell, Paradise, / Or to oblivion?"

Spiritual Acrylic Expressionism
